<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><em>Make smarter, faster, and more confident quote decisions—right where you work. </em></p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Project quoting has always&nbsp;required&nbsp;a careful balance—aligning profitability with competitiveness, staffing strategies with delivery costs, and customer expectations with business outcomes.&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">But evaluating these trade-offs&nbsp;hasn’t&nbsp;always been easy. It often means jumping between tools, manually recalculating numbers, and relying on assumptions to guide critical decisions.&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">That’s where <strong>What-if Analysis (Preview) in Dynamics 365 Project Operations</strong> comes in. </p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This new capability brings real-time simulation directly into your quoting workflow—so you can explore options, compare outcomes, and make decisions with clarity before&nbsp;finalizing&nbsp;a quote.&nbsp;</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading" id="what-is-what-if-analysis"><strong>What Is&nbsp;What-if&nbsp;Analysis?</strong>&nbsp;</h4>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">What-if&nbsp;Analysis introduces a dedicated simulation workspace within a project quote, allowing you to model changes to quantities and pricing and instantly see their&nbsp;financial impact.&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Instead of working through “what if” scenarios offline, you can now:&nbsp;</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list has-body-medium-font-size">
<li class="wp-block-list-item">Explore multiple approaches within the quote </li>
</ul>



<ul class="wp-block-list has-body-medium-font-size">
<li class="wp-block-list-item">Compare their outcomes side by side </li>
</ul>



<ul class="wp-block-list has-body-medium-font-size">
<li class="wp-block-list-item">Apply the most effective scenario when you&#8217;re ready </li>
</ul>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">All without&nbsp;modifying&nbsp;the actual quote until you choose to.&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">It’s&nbsp;a more intuitive, controlled way to move from estimation to decision-making.&nbsp;</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading" id="turn-everyday-questions-into-clear-answers"><strong>Turn Everyday Questions into Clear Answers</strong>&nbsp;</h4>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Every project quote involves key decisions:&nbsp;</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list has-body-medium-font-size">
<li class="wp-block-list-item">Should work shift to a lower-cost delivery center? </li>
</ul>



<ul class="wp-block-list has-body-medium-font-size">
<li class="wp-block-list-item">What happens if billing rates increase for specific roles? </li>
</ul>



<ul class="wp-block-list has-body-medium-font-size">
<li class="wp-block-list-item">Can you stay competitive while protecting margin? </li>
</ul>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">With What-if Analysis, these are no longer hypothetical questions.&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">As you&nbsp;adjust&nbsp;quantities and pricing, the system instantly recalculates key financial metrics—including revenue, cost, gross margin, and budget&nbsp;variance—so you can clearly see the impact of every change.&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This real-time feedback helps you move quickly from exploration to confident, data-backed decisions.&nbsp;</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading" id="how-it-works"><strong>How It Works</strong>&nbsp;</h4>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Getting started is simple. From the&nbsp;<strong>What-if Analysis tab</strong>&nbsp;on a Draft quote, you can create a scenario based on the quote’s existing data. Each scenario is isolated, allowing you to experiment freely without affecting the live quote.&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Within the simulation workspace, you can adjust quantities and pricing across dimensions such as resourcing unit, role, or any custom pricing dimension configured in your environment. Whether&nbsp;you’re&nbsp;making high-level adjustments or refining details at the quote line level, the experience is designed to be flexible and intuitive.&nbsp;</p>


<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img alt="" class="wp-image-201781 webp-format" src="https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/dynamics-365/blog/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/image-29-1024x607.webp" /></figure>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">You can create multiple scenarios—each&nbsp;representing&nbsp;a different approach—and compare them side by side. Built-in comparison views highlight differences in financial outcomes, making trade-offs easier to evaluate.&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">When&nbsp;you’ve&nbsp;identified&nbsp;the best approach, applying the scenario updates the Draft quote in place—so you can move forward with confidence, without creating a new revision.&nbsp;</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading" id="what-this-means-for-you"><strong>What This&nbsp;Means for You</strong>&nbsp;</h4>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">What-if&nbsp;Analysis transforms&nbsp;how&nbsp;you approach project&nbsp;quoting—bringing clarity, speed, and confidence into every decision.&nbsp;</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list has-body-medium-font-size">
<li class="wp-block-list-item"><strong>Make decisions with confidence:</strong> Instantly understand how pricing and staffing changes impact revenue, cost, and margin—before committing to a quote </li>
</ul>



<ul class="wp-block-list has-body-medium-font-size">
<li class="wp-block-list-item"><strong>Optimize for both competitiveness and profitability:</strong> Evaluate trade-offs in real time and choose the approach that best aligns with your goals </li>
</ul>



<ul class="wp-block-list has-body-medium-font-size">
<li class="wp-block-list-item"><strong>Reduce reliance on spreadsheets and manual iteration:</strong> Keep simulation and decision-making within Project Operations </li>
</ul>



<ul class="wp-block-list has-body-medium-font-size">
<li class="wp-block-list-item"><strong>Drive faster, more aligned conversations:</strong> Use data-backed scenarios to align stakeholders and move decisions forward </li>
</ul>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Instead of relying on assumptions, your team can now explore possibilities, evaluate outcomes, and&nbsp;finalize&nbsp;quotes with confidence—knowing the numbers&nbsp;support&nbsp;the decision.&nbsp;</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading" id="availability-and-prerequisites"><strong>Availability and Prerequisites</strong>&nbsp;</h4>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">What-if Analysis is currently available as a&nbsp;<strong>preview feature</strong>&nbsp;in:&nbsp;</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li class="wp-block-list-item">Project Operations Core (Lite deployment) </li>
</ul>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li class="wp-block-list-item">Project Operations integrated with ERP </li>
</ul>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">To get started, enable the What-if Analysis feature flag in your environment. The What-if Analysis tab will then be available on qualifying Draft quotes.&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">A few things to keep in mind:&nbsp;</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list has-body-medium-font-size">
<li class="wp-block-list-item">Scenarios can only be created on quotes in Draft status that contain estimates </li>
</ul>



<ul class="wp-block-list has-body-medium-font-size">
<li class="wp-block-list-item">Activated or closed quotes are not eligible </li>
</ul>



<ul class="wp-block-list has-body-medium-font-size">
<li class="wp-block-list-item">If the underlying quote changes, scenarios will need to be recreated </li>
</ul>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">As with all preview features, we recommend evaluating this capability in a non-production environment.&nbsp;</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading" id="the-bottom-line"><strong>The Bottom Line</strong>&nbsp;</h4>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Every project quote is a critical business decision. What-if Analysis gives you the tools to approach that decision with&nbsp;clarity—replacing guesswork with real-time insight and manual effort with seamless simulation.&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The result is not&nbsp;just better&nbsp;quotes, but better&nbsp;decisions—ones&nbsp;that are competitive, financially sound, and aligned with your business goals.&nbsp;</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading" id="get-started"><strong>Get Started</strong>&nbsp;</h4>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Enable What-if Analysis in your environment today and start turning “what if?” into “we know.”&nbsp;</p>
