<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Modern customer experiences don’t live in a single system or interaction type. Whether agents resolve complex cases in <strong>Dynamics Customer Service</strong> or manage live conversations in <strong>Dynamics Contact Center</strong>, the quality of an interaction often depends on how the work is performed and not just what was said. </p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Across both workloads, customers have consistently asked for the same missing capability:&nbsp;<strong>secure, structured visibility into the agent’s&nbsp;on</strong>‑<strong>screen&nbsp;workflow during customer interactions</strong>. Voice, chat, and transcripts capture part of the story, but they&nbsp;don’t&nbsp;always reveal how agents navigate systems, apply processes, or respond in real time.&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Today,&nbsp;<strong>Screen Recording in Dynamics 365 Customer Service and Dynamics 365 Contact Center</strong>&nbsp;addresses that gap&nbsp;by&nbsp;providing organizations with trusted,&nbsp;role‑based&nbsp;access to screen context that supports coaching, quality evaluation, and compliance.&nbsp;</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="why-screen-context-matters"><strong>Why Screen Context Matters</strong>&nbsp;</h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Customer interactions are rarely linear. Agents often work across multiple systems, reference internal knowledge, update records, and follow prescribed processes,&nbsp;all while engaging with the customer.&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Although Customer Service and Contact Center support different interaction models, customers using both workloads face remarkably similar challenges:&nbsp;</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li class="wp-block-list-item">Supervisors need better context to evaluate agent’s activities when helping customers.&nbsp;</li>
</ul>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li class="wp-block-list-item">Compliance teams require auditable visibility in regulated environments.&nbsp;</li>
</ul>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li class="wp-block-list-item">Organizations want to coach agents more effectively without guesswork.&nbsp;</li>
</ul>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Without screen context, organizations must infer intent and execution. With it, teams can: </p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li class="wp-block-list-item">Perform more&nbsp;accurate&nbsp;and objective quality evaluations&nbsp;</li>
</ul>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li class="wp-block-list-item">Identify&nbsp;training gaps and process inconsistencies&nbsp;</li>
</ul>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li class="wp-block-list-item">Reduce time to resolution by understanding workflow breakdowns&nbsp;</li>
</ul>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li class="wp-block-list-item">Validate compliance and&nbsp;required&nbsp;actions with confidence&nbsp;</li>
</ul>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Screen&nbsp;recording&nbsp;complements existing call and chat records, enabling a more complete understanding of each interaction,&nbsp;regardless of&nbsp;the&nbsp;channel.&nbsp;</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="one-capability-designed-for-two-workloads"><strong>One Capability, Designed for Two Workloads</strong>&nbsp;</h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">We&#8217;ve built Screen Recording in Dynamics 365 as a unified capability that serves both Customer Service and Contact Center, while adapting naturally to different ways agents work. </p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading" id="key-capabilities">Key Capabilities:&nbsp;</h4>



<ol class="wp-block-list" start="1">
<li class="wp-block-list-item">Automatic Screen Recording for Call Conversations&nbsp;</li>
</ol>



<ol class="wp-block-list" start="2">
<li class="wp-block-list-item">Manual Screen Recording for service reps working on multiple cases, chats, etc.&nbsp;&nbsp;</li>
</ol>



<ol class="wp-block-list" start="3">
<li class="wp-block-list-item">All screen recordings are securely uploaded to Dataverse&nbsp;&nbsp;</li>
</ol>



<ol class="wp-block-list" start="4">
<li class="wp-block-list-item">Role based access to both perform and access screen recordings&nbsp;</li>
</ol>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Manual screen recording when service reps are working on cases, for example:&nbsp;</p>


<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img alt="" class="wp-image-202082 webp-format" src="https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/dynamics-365/blog/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/image-1-1.webp" /></figure>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">To balance visibility with usability, recording is scoped intentionally, and configuration and operational details are managed by administrators.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>


<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img alt="" class="wp-image-202081 webp-format" src="https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/dynamics-365/blog/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/image-4.webp" /></figure>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">For&nbsp;step‑by‑step&nbsp;setup and configuration guidance, detailed documentation is available on&nbsp;<a href="https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/dynamics365/contact-center/administer/configure-screen-recording?branch=main" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">Microsoft Learn</a>.&nbsp;</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="secure-by-design-trust-access-and-governance"><strong>Secure by Design: Trust, Access, and Governance</strong>&nbsp;</h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Because screen recordings may&nbsp;contain&nbsp;sensitive information, security and governance were foundational to the design.&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Access is controlled through&nbsp;role‑based&nbsp;permissions,&nbsp;ensuring only authorized users can record, review, or download recordings. Recordings are stored securely, with controls that align to enterprise and regulatory expectations.&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This approach allows organizations to unlock operational insight without compromising trust&nbsp;between agents, supervisors, and compliance teams.&nbsp;</p>
