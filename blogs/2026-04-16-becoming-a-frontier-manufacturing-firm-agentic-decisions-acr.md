<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Manufacturing leaders today are under constant pressure: volatile demand, fragile supply networks, inventory risk, and zero tolerance for missed customer commitments. Plants must protect margins and&nbsp;maintain&nbsp;uptime while responding to disruption with incomplete or delayed information.&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">To lead in this environment, manufacturers need decision-making that surfaces risk earlier,&nbsp;absorb&nbsp;supply shocks faster, and replans quickly, so production stays aligned to demand and customer commitments.&nbsp;</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="microsoft-dynamics-365-is-your-agentic-enterprise-resource-planning-erp-foundation"><strong>Microsoft&nbsp;Dynamics 365&nbsp;is your agentic enterprise resource planning (ERP) foundation</strong></h2>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><a href="https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/dynamics-365" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">Dynamics 365</a>&nbsp;gives&nbsp;manufacturers a connected foundation where signals from demand, supply, capacity, and cost can drive action. As part of the Microsoft platform, it brings together data, analytics, automation, and collaboration across the business.&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Imagine a manufacturing stack where every part of the business is connected, where data&nbsp;isn’t&nbsp;fragmented across systems but unified into a shared understanding of operations, and where insights&nbsp;don’t&nbsp;sit in dashboards but show up in context, in the&nbsp;<a href="https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/microsoft-365/blog/2026/04/13/bring-your-everyday-business-apps-into-the-flow-of-work-with-agents-in-microsoft-365-copilot/" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">flow of work</a>. Where your teams can build AI agents and adapt processes in real time rather than reengineering&nbsp;them months&nbsp;later. Where every person&nbsp;operates&nbsp;with the full context of the business,&nbsp;demand, supply, capacity, and commitments,&nbsp;every decision drives aligned action across the company. This is what it means to close the gap between knowing and doing.&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">At&nbsp;<a href="https://www.hannovermesse.de/veranstaltung/from-insight-to-action-the-rise-of-agentic-supply-chains-in-manufacturing/ses/99904" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">Hannover Messe 2026</a>, Microsoft is&nbsp;showcasing&nbsp;how agentic ERP&nbsp;with&nbsp;<a href="https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/microsoft-365-copilot" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">Copilot</a>, agents and Dynamics 365&nbsp;supports&nbsp;these operational decisions&nbsp;amid constant change, helping manufacturers replan faster, make better production tradeoffs, and meet or exceed customer service SLAs.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="agentic-demand-planning-can-sense-shifts-earlier"><strong>Agentic&nbsp;demand planning&nbsp;can&nbsp;sense&nbsp;shifts earlier&nbsp;</strong>&nbsp;</h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Demand&nbsp;planning&nbsp;can help you forecast&nbsp;demand,&nbsp;applying a structured phase in/phase out with external signals to account for market dynamics.&nbsp;Copilot accelerates&nbsp;this&nbsp;data&nbsp;analysis, scenario modeling, and incorporates market intelligence.&nbsp;For structured planning,&nbsp;<a href="https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/dynamics365/supply-chain/demand-planning/demand-planning-home-page" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">Demand Planning</a>&nbsp;in&nbsp;<a href="https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/dynamics-365/products/supply-chain-management" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">Dynamics 365 Supply Chain Management</a>&nbsp;provides an&nbsp;agentic&nbsp;collaborative demand planning solution, powered by best-in-class forecasting algorithms and models, an immersive user experience with AI, intelligent&nbsp;segmentation and signal analysis, and analytics.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="agent-evaluates-supplier-disruption-impact-enabling-operational-resilience"><strong>Agent&nbsp;evaluates&nbsp;supplier disruption impact&nbsp;enabling operational resilience</strong>&nbsp;</h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">A delayed component or supplier exception is not a paperwork problem. It is a production and operational risk. <strong>Today, Microsoft announced the Procurement Agent in Dynamics 365 Supply Chain Management is available in public preview.</strong> The Procurement Agent helps teams respond faster to supply disruption by decoding supplier communications and identifying which production orders, inventory positions, and customer commitments are at risk, while keeping humans in control. Beyond triaging supplier communications, the Procurement Agent performs impact analysis so teams can assess downstream effects and act before a supplier issue turns into an on time, in full (OTIF) failure.</p>

<cite>Sean Barrett, Inventory and Analytics Manager,&nbsp;Farmlands Cooperative&nbsp;| <a href="https://www.microsoft.com/en/customers/story/26293-farmlands-cooperative-dynamics-365-commerce">Read more about Farmlands Cooperative: Sowing the seeds of innovation with autonomous agents in Dynamics 365</a></cite></blockquote>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="agent-mitigates-production-constraints"><strong>Agent&nbsp;mitigates production constraints</strong>&nbsp;</h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">For the shop floor, Copilot, connected to Dynamics 365 Supply Chain Management via&nbsp;<a href="https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/dynamics365/fin-ops-core/dev-itpro/copilot/copilot-mcp" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">Dynamics 365 MCP Server</a>&nbsp;(Model Context Protocol), can&nbsp;provide&nbsp;AI-generated insights to schedules, inventory, and orders. Copilot can also surface&nbsp;quality-related alerts&nbsp;and&nbsp;can suggest&nbsp;targeted inspections&nbsp;to prevent recurring recalibration issues&nbsp;enabling supervisors to proactively mitigate resource constraints and shortages before they&nbsp;impact&nbsp;production schedules, triggering downstream inventory repositioning and order promise adjustments across fulfillment and customer commitments.&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Dynamics 365 Supply Chain Management provides near real-time visibility to the production floor; with agentic experiences, supervisors can spend less time gathering data and more time on proactive exception management. This translates to higher Overall Equipment Effectiveness (OEE), fewer unplanned stops, and better on-time delivery to customers.&nbsp;</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="agent-protects-production-uptime"><strong>Agent&nbsp;protects production uptime</strong>&nbsp;</h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Equipment uptime and asset health are critical inputs to manufacturing performance. Unplanned downtime, delayed service, or poorly coordinated technician schedules can quickly cascade into constrained capacity and missed production targets. The&nbsp;<a href="https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/dynamics365/field-service/soa-overview" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">Scheduling Operations Agent</a>&nbsp;in Dynamics 365 Field Service&nbsp;can&nbsp;help&nbsp;maintain&nbsp;equipment availability for constrained production schedules by dynamically coordinating technician assignments based on geography, skills, certification requirements, capacity, travel time, and SLA commitments.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Today, Microsoft announced Researcher in Microsoft 365 Copilot is available in public preview within Dynamics 365 Field Service.</strong> With Microsoft 365 Copilot in <a href="https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/dynamics-365/products/field-service" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">Dynamics 365 Field Service</a>, manufacturers can use Researcher to quickly view operational information across work orders, asset and service history, and parts availability. Researcher surfaces relevant context from Field Service data and Microsoft 365 signals to help teams understand situations as they arise.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="agentic-warehouse-management-can-fulfill-demand-faster"><strong>Agentic&nbsp;warehouse&nbsp;management can&nbsp;fulfill demand faster&nbsp;</strong>&nbsp;</h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">As finished goods hit the&nbsp;warehouse,&nbsp;intelligent&nbsp;inventory&nbsp;on-hand balancing in Dynamics 365 Supply Chain Management warehouse management updates slotting to align&nbsp;demand with pick zones. When&nbsp;inbound&nbsp;put-away happens, workers are directed to the best locations based on&nbsp;the intelligent&nbsp;inventory balancing, keeping the warehouse organized dynamically. On the&nbsp;outbound&nbsp;side, optimized pick routes shorten travel distance. The combined outcome is higher throughput&nbsp;with the same labor—faster fulfillment, lower picking cost.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<blockquote class="wp-block-quote has-quote-default-font-size is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong><em>With Dynamics 365, we trust the system.&nbsp;It&#8217;s&nbsp;easy to retrieve information and know&nbsp;it’s&nbsp;correct; people can just drive the forklift over.&nbsp;All&nbsp;the considerations we have for our picking, packing, and customer details, the system is doing it for us.</em></strong>&nbsp;</p>
<cite><strong>Niclas Strand, Warehouse Manager,&nbsp;KåKå, Orkla Food</strong>&nbsp;| <a href="https://www.microsoft.com/en/customers/story/22107-orkla-dynamics-365-finance">Read more about why Orkla Food Ingredients’ Dynamics 365 transformation is a sweet upgrade</a></cite></blockquote>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="agentic-b2b-commerce-aligns-customer-orders-to-production-reality"><strong>Agentic B2B&nbsp;Commerce&nbsp;aligns customer orders to production reality</strong>&nbsp;</h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">As manufacturers and industrial distributors modernize how they sell, serve, and scale, digital ordering is becoming a core capability of the ERP rather than a standalone channel. Dynamics 365 Commerce enables manufacturers to support complex buying and selling models including&nbsp;business-to-business (B2B), business-to-business-to-business (B2B2B), and&nbsp;multioutlet&nbsp;ordering across direct customers, distributors, and dealer networks.&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Agentic&nbsp;B2B&nbsp;commerce&nbsp;can&nbsp;help automate routine ordering, replenishment, and quote-to-order&nbsp;processes while remaining tightly connected to pricing, inventory, warehousing, and production planning. This&nbsp;can&nbsp;allow manufacturers and distributors to accelerate order cycles, strengthen partner collaboration, and scale indirect growth with accuracy and control.&nbsp;</p>
